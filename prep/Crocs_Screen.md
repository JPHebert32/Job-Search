## Likely Questions + STAR Answers

### 1. "Tell me about yourself."
> "I'm a Senior BI specialist — 10 years owning the full pipeline from Snowflake queries to executive Power BI dashboards. Most recently I was the sole BI analyst on T-Life, T-Mobile's super app with 75 million downloads. I owned all 30 dashboards, built the semantic models and KPI framework from scratch, and delivered to VPs and Directors daily with C-suite access. I'm the person who builds the foundational model that other teams rely on — and I also do the training and documentation so it doesn't fall apart when I'm not in the room. That's exactly what this role is asking for."

### 2. "Walk me through a complex Power BI solution you built end-to-end."
Use T-Life HEART framework — leads directly into the JD requirements.

> **Situation:** T-Life had no unified BI infrastructure. Multiple product teams, no standard models, no shared KPIs.
> **Task:** Build the measurement platform from zero as the sole analyst.
> **Action:** Built the full semantic model in Power BI backed by Snowflake and Databricks queries — normalized, documented, and versioned. Implemented Google's HEART framework across 7 dashboard sections, plus 23 product-level reports. Established the data definitions, managed access across all product teams, and set standards for how new reports got built.
> **Result:** 30 dashboards, 100+ daily visits, VP and C-suite access, source of truth for the entire T-Life program. In the final 6 months, trained incoming analysts and documented the full build so they could take ownership without breaking anything.

*This directly addresses: semantic model ownership, documentation, mentoring, and multi-stakeholder delivery.*

### 3. "How do you support non-technical stakeholders?"
> "I've delivered to VPs and Senior Directors every day for 5 years. The rule I use is: the dashboard should answer the question before they ask it. That means knowing what decisions get made off these reports and designing the layout around the decision flow, not the data structure. At T-Mobile, I ran monthly demos where I'd walk leadership through what had changed, why it changed, and what actions were available. I also built a daily VP summary report at T-Sentiment — had to be clean enough that the VP could read it in 3 minutes before a meeting."

### 4. "Tell me about training users on Power BI tools."
> "In my last 6 months at T-Mobile, my primary job was knowledge transfer. I trained the incoming analysts on the T-Life dashboard suite — documented data definitions, model structure, refresh schedules, and build conventions. The goal was that they could maintain and extend the dashboards without introducing drift. I also acted informally as a Power BI resource for product managers who wanted to understand how to read the data, not just see the output."

### 5. "Why are you leaving your last role?"
> "ProCogia had a consulting contract with T-Mobile. T-Mobile eliminated contract and remote roles in April — I was part of that reduction. I'd actually been in knowledge-transfer mode for 6 months preparing the team to take over, so the transition was clean. I'm looking for a direct role where I own the infrastructure long-term."
