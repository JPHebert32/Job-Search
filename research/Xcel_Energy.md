## What they do
Xcel Energy (NYSE: XEL) is a regulated electric and natural gas utility serving 8 states (Colorado, Minnesota, Texas, New Mexico, and others), providing power to approximately 3.7 million electric and 2.1 million natural gas customers.

## Stage & funding
- **Public company (NYSE: XEL):** Market cap ~$30–35B (unverified, utility sector)
- **Regulated utility:** Revenue and margins are set by state Public Utilities Commissions (PUCs), not market dynamics
- **$60B capital spending plan:** [Utility Dive reported](https://www.utilitydive.com/news/xcel-energy-rolls-out-60-billion-capital-spending-plan/804355/) Xcel rolled out a $60B infrastructure investment plan — renewable energy transition, grid modernization, large-scale capital projects
- **Financial health:** Stable and predictable — regulated utilities don't go bankrupt; revenue is near-guaranteed by government rate-setting

## Business model
Regulated monopoly: generates, transmits, and distributes electricity and natural gas. Revenue comes from rate-regulated tariffs approved by state PUCs. Growth comes from infrastructure investment (rate base expansion) and new customer connections, not from market competition or product innovation.

## Questions to ask if you engage the recruiter
1. What is the comp band for this specific Data Analyst role — is it at or above $115K?
2. Is this role on any on-call rotation, even occasional? (Deal-breaker check)
3. What's the remote/hybrid arrangement — full remote, hybrid, or in-office?
4. What data platform is this team using — are they on Databricks, SQL Server, or something else?
5. Which department does this role sit in — grid operations, finance, customer analytics?

## Red flags / yellow flags
- **RED — On-call:** Glassdoor explicitly cites 6-week on-call rotations with 12-hour shifts. This is a deal-breaker per your stated criteria. Confirm with recruiter before proceeding.
- **RED — Comp ceiling:** Published salary range tops at ~$115.8K — at your absolute floor. Average is ~$89K. No upside.
- **RED — F500 bureaucracy:** Regulated utility with 8,000+ employees, political promotion culture, "stale" corporate environment — directly contradicts your preference for Series B–pre-IPO stage.
- **RED — Industry mismatch:** Utilities analytics (grid, emissions, regulatory) does not translate to product analytics roles. Could be a résumé silo.
- **Yellow — Recruiter-initiated:** Recruiter reached out, which usually means urgency to fill — but the role may be hard to fill for a reason (see above).
- **Green — Stability:** Zero layoff risk; Xcel will not go bankrupt. Job security is exceptional.
- **Green — Denver/hybrid:** Denver-based with some remote flexibility.

**Recommendation: Do not pursue. Three deal-breakers overlap simultaneously: on-call rotation, below-floor comp, and F500 bureaucracy. If you respond to the recruiter, do so to politely decline — don't invest time interviewing unless they confirm no on-call and $120K+.**
