# Leprino Foods — Research Brief
*Prepared: 2026-04-27 | Role: Business Intelligence Analyst II | Location: Denver, CO | Applied: 2026-04-23 via LinkedIn*

## What they do
Leprino Foods is the world's largest mozzarella cheese manufacturer, supplying Pizza Hut, Domino's, Papa John's, and most major pizza chains globally from its Denver, CO headquarters.

## Stage & funding
- **Ownership:** 100% private, family-owned since 1950 — founded by Jim Leprino, still controlled by the Leprino family
- **No external investors, no funding rounds, no IPO planned** — this is a family business, not a VC-backed company
- **Revenue:** Widely cited at $5–6B annually (unverified, not publicly disclosed); ZoomInfo/LeadIQ estimates vary widely and are unreliable for private companies
- **Employees:** ~5,000+ across multiple US manufacturing plants

## Business model
Sells dairy ingredients (mozzarella, whey protein, lactose) at scale to QSR chains and food manufacturers. B2B ingredients model, not consumer-facing. Revenue is volume-driven and tied to long-term supply contracts.

- **[$1B Lubbock TX plant expansion](https://www.zoominfo.com/c/leprino-foods/29835955):** Phase 2 of a major new manufacturing facility coming online in early 2026 — significant internal tech/data infrastructure needs expected as operations scale

## Data team signals

- **Google Analytics** listed in their web tools

- The BI Analyst II role is in Denver corporate HQ — likely supporting finance, supply chain, or operations analytics, not product analytics
- The $1B Lubbock expansion suggests internal BI pressure around manufacturing and supply chain data

## Red flags / yellow flags
- **Yellow — Industry mismatch:** This is food manufacturing, not B2B SaaS/fintech. The BI work is likely supply chain, manufacturing, or finance-focused rather than product analytics.
- **Yellow — Tech stack:** SAP + Oracle suggests a mature, possibly legacy data environment. No evidence of modern stack (dbt, Snowflake, etc.).
- **Yellow — Culture:** Conservative family-owned culture with documented management friction. Outsiders can struggle.
- **Yellow — WLB:** 2.9/5 on WLB is below average. Long hours appear common.
- **Green — Stability:** No layoff risk, strong cash position, major expansion investment. Job security is solid.
- **Green — Denver:** HQ role in Denver, likely hybrid-friendly.
