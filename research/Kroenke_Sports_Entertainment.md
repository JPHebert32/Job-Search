# Kroenke Sports & Entertainment — Research Brief
*Prepared: 2026-04-27 | Role: Business Intelligence Analyst | Location: Denver, CO | Status: Reviewing (visited Lensa, not yet applied)*

## What they do
Kroenke Sports & Entertainment (KSE) is a privately owned sports and entertainment conglomerate owning the Denver Nuggets (NBA), Colorado Avalanche (NHL), Colorado Rapids (MLS), Colorado Rockies (MLB), LA Rams (NFL), Arsenal FC (Premier League), and Ball Arena / Empower Field venues.

## Stage & funding
- **Ownership:** Privately held by Stan Kroenke (net worth ~$16B); no external investors, no IPO planned
- **No public financials** — revenues are undisclosed; estimated $1B+ annually across all properties (unverified)
- **Revenue model:** Ticket sales, media rights, sponsorships, arena F&B, merchandise across multiple pro sports franchises

## Business model
Multi-franchise sports/entertainment revenue: live event attendance, national and local broadcast rights deals, corporate sponsorships, premium hospitality, and venue operations. Data and analytics directly support ticket pricing, fan segmentation, sponsor valuation, and commercial revenue optimization.

## Questions to ask in the interview
1. What is the primary data platform — are you building beyond KORE, and is there a Snowflake or cloud warehouse layer?
2. The new SVP of Analytics just joined — what's his vision for the BI function over the next 12 months?
3. What KPIs will this BI Analyst own — fan segmentation, ticket pricing, sponsor reporting, or something else?
4. What's the comp band for this role, and is there a 401K match?
5. What does a typical week look like — is this a standard M–F cadence, or does live event schedule affect work hours?

## Red flags / yellow flags
- **Yellow — Industry mismatch:** Sports/entertainment is outside your stated target (B2B SaaS, fintech, marketplaces). The analytics work is fan-data and revenue-ops focused, not product analytics.
- **Yellow — Comp risk:** Reviews consistently flag below-market pay and no 401K match. Verify the band against your $115K–$140K target before applying.
- **Yellow — Live event schedule:** Last-minute long hours are cited frequently — tied to game nights and events, not a traditional 9-5.
- **Yellow — Tech stack:** KORE Software is sports-specific, not a transferable modern data stack. Could limit your next move.
- **Green — Momentum:** New SVP Analytics + new CCO hired in April 2026 = active team buildout. Good time to join.
- **Green — Denver:** HQ role, likely in-person at Ball Arena/Denver office. Aligns with location.
- **Green — Unique brand:** Working across Nuggets, Avalanche, Arsenal, Rams is compelling on a resume.

**Recommendation: Worth a call to learn the comp band and tech stack. If pay meets your floor and the stack has transferable elements, it's worth pursuing — especially with new analytics leadership actively hiring.**
