## What they do
Shutterfly is a direct-to-consumer photo products and personalized gifts company (photo books, cards, prints, home décor) — the largest online photo printing platform in the US.

## Stage & funding
- **Ownership:** Private — acquired by Apollo Global Management in 2019 for $2.7 billion, taken off NYSE
- **Debt load:** Apollo acquisition was highly leveraged; debt has constrained investment since
- **Bankruptcy:** Filed Chapter 11 in 2023, emerged post-restructuring — Apollo retained ownership
- **Revenue:** Declining — from ~$2.1B (2023) to an estimated ~$1.8B (2025) ([Tracxn](https://tracxn.com/d/companies/shutterfly/__FM_Vp3HPxwXLPbTty80dHB9NIeWyeeGVjU1B4_98EWc))
- **New CFO:** Incoming as of early 2026 — ex-Philips exec replacing departed CFO ([CFO Dive](https://www.cfodive.com/news/shutterfly-cfo-steps-down-ex-philips-exec-take-over-teuwen/693062/))

## Business model
Consumer e-commerce: customers upload photos, Shutterfly manufactures and ships personalized products. Revenue is seasonal (heavy Q4/holiday), subscription-plus-transaction hybrid. B2C, not SaaS.
